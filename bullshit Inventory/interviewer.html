Can you really interview 100,000 people a year?

Date: 10-04-2017

Source: Bullshit I debunked; Business Insider (view source)

Summary

        A title "I interview 100,000 people a year, and there are 6 questions that make job candidates stick in my mind" came into my sight when I was checking my Facebook the other day.

Argument

        I thought of the title for a minute and confirmed that this is bullshit because it does not care about the truth and and mutates the fact. It is a simple calculation: assuming he works 12 hours everyday without taking a single holiday, he has to interview 23 candidates for 365 days! Obviously, this cannot be fact.

Conclusion

        This is bullshit because the statement does not care about the truth and mutates the fact. It is to the public, so the magnitude of BS is 3 when everything added up. However, I would say the intention is good because the article wants to give job candidates some advice.
