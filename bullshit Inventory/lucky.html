"Sorry, I am just lucky today."

Date: 10-08-2017

Source: Bullshit I produced myself.

Summary

        On last Saturday, I played basketbll in IMA. I played one-on-one with a dude. I beat him twice and I kind of noticed he was not very happy about it. In order to make him feel better, I told him "sorry bro, I am just lucky today. I do not usually shoot this well." He, then, laughed and seemed feel better (lol). But did I really just make those shots by luck? Not really. I thought I shot like my average level.

Argument

        Clearly, I lied of my belief for good reason. But did I mutate the fact or was I careless about truth? Neither. I believed that I performed my average level, but the fact may or may not be that I did shoot better than my average level. It is just hard to tell if my belief equals to the fact. Therefore, I did not mutate the fact because I did not know about the fact. Furthermore, it is not that I did not care about the truth. Rather, I just lied so to not make my opponent feel bad.

Conclusion

        I lied of my belief for good intention, so the magnitude of this statement is 1 and the intention of the statement is positve.
